Sezam — Doors & Gates

A public-facing website for Sezam, a Kharkiv business in metal doors and gates. The site presents the product range, builds trust for local buyers, and makes it easy to get in touch—optimized for phones, quick loading, and clear structure.

HTML CSS JavaScript Responsive UX
Live site GitHub

Overview

Sezam serves customers looking for metal doors and gates in Kharkiv. The website’s job is to explain what they offer, show quality and professionalism, and turn visits into calls or messages—without overwhelming users with clutter.

The build focuses on a straightforward information architecture: who Sezam is, what products and services matter, and how to contact them. Copy and layout support a B2C and small-business audience that often browses on mobile.

Problem

Local trades and installation businesses often lose leads when their web presence is slow, hard to read on phones, or fails to answer basic questions (range, service area, how to order). Visitors decide in seconds whether to stay or leave.

The challenge was to reflect a credible, established brand online: clear visuals hierarchy, scannable sections, and contact paths that work equally well for first-time visitors and returning clients.

Solution

A marketing site with responsive layouts, consistent typography and spacing, and emphasis on product categories and trust signals suited to construction and home-improvement buyers.

Performance and accessibility basics (readable contrast, tap targets, semantic structure) help both users and search visibility for local queries around doors, gates, and Kharkiv.

Architecture

Typical flow from discovery to contact: the visitor lands on the site, navigates content, and reaches a clear call-to-action (phone, form, or messenger, depending on how the live site is configured).
